# The Mirror

The Mirror is a timer designed to help you train in presence and awareness, as defined in the
Dzogchen tradition of Tibetan Buddhism. It is named after Namkhai Norbu Rinpoche's book of the same
name. The terms are defined as follows:

- Presence (*smṛti* in Sanskrit, *dran pa* in Tibetan): It means memory or recollection, the act of
  bringing the mind back to its intended focus. This word is commonly translated as "mindfulness".
- Awareness (*samprajanya* in Sanskrit, *shes bzhin* in Tibetan): It is the mental faculty that
  stabilizes memory upon the object and guards against distraction.

Presence and awareness always work as a pair. If you are a Dzogchen practitioner, you use presence
and awareness to remain in the state of instant presence (*rig pa* in Tibetan). However, this app
can be used by anyone who needs help training in sustaining presence and awareness throughout the
day, such as lucid dreamers or practitioners of other traditions.

## Basic Usage

Select a set of quotes and sound of your choice, and then start the timer. A notification will be
set up. When it goes off, a quote will be displayed and the sound will be played. By clicking or
holding the notification, you will have the option to enter whether you have been present or
distracted from the last time the timer went off.

The timer is not meant to simply provide periodic reminders to be present, but to help you train in
sustaining presence and awareness throughout the day. For that reason, it is important that you
provide honest feedback on whether you have been present or distracted and actively attempt to
sustain presence and awareness.

If you have been present, the interval until the next notification will be doubled. If you have been
distracted, the interval will be halved. The interval can range from 5 to 90 minutes, with some
random jitter added to prevent the timer from becoming too predictable.

To pause the timer during sleep or other periods when it's not needed, you can either stop the timer
by clicking on the "Stop" button or by configuring the "Do Not Disturb" mode in your iPhone settings
to automatically mute the notifications during certain hours.
